KARBYTES_JOURNAL_2022_ENTRY_145

I had a miserable night because that Pig Gorl voice was the most obnoxious and antagonistic I have ever seen it be.

I noticed that Pig Gorl is most prominent when I am in Castro Valley; especially the bathrooms at The Lost Ridge campsite north of Lake Chabot. For that reason, I have decided that I will have to live in Dublin because the voices there are not as disruptive to my goals.

What I have noticed about Pig Gorl is that she is extremely anti science. Whenever I try to change the subject of what I am thinking about to something other than her bullying, she gets louder, more frantic, talks faster, and more multi-tracked. 100% she says is some kind of taunt or insult. She sounds like a bratty young child pretending to be a childcare expert. She whimpers, whines, nags, whisper yells, makes breathy glottal stop sounds, and coos antagonistically. She seems obsessed with preventing me from studying and forcing me to listen to repetitive, anxiety mongering, unkind, and shallow garbage as punishment for not making family the center of my life and for having interests which are not exclusively feminine (and feminine interests pertain exclusively to making sexuality, marriage, motherhood, idle gossip, food preparation, hospitality, interior home design, and beauty treatments).

“Get over yourself,” and “No one wants to listen to you,” and “Bye Karlina” are typical phrases Pig Gorl chortles. If I could summarize her agenda in one sentence it would be this: “You are not loved by enough people and you do not make other people the center of your life enough for anyone to want to invest in you. Hence, you deserve to be prevented from pursuing your goals and you deserve to be psychologically tortured for the rest of your life. You deserve to be treated as an irredeemable felon and exile to your home town. You do not deserve to be happy. You are only allowed to be in excruciating agony over having to renounce your most cherished plans and ideals while others smugly watch and rejoice in your demise. Your happiness is not essential enough people for it to be legal.”

I wanted to make this chapter the first real green bin chapter in this website. So far, most of the individual journal entries in this chapter are labeled as pink (i.e. (-)) rather than as green (i.e. (+)). If the number of pink journal entries in this chapter is larger than the number of green journal entries, I will move this chapter to the pink bin section of this website. I think that, once I relocate to Dublin as my default camping spot, it will be easier for me to generate more green content than pink content.

I have noticed many times that Castro Valley is a hard place for me to be in for more than a few hours at a time. I think that is because I am not as family centric as most other Castro Valley residents are. It’s a very insular place. It does not feel like part of the larger encompassing Bay Area. Instead, it feels like a small town in the middle of the heartland of the United States. It seems very conservative Christian, culturally homogeneous, xenophobic, and anti modern. It seems like a retreat away from what Silicon Valley and Oakland, Berkeley, and San Francisco are all about; a place which feels frozen in time for the comfort of the sleeping babies which arise the only commodities Castro Valley seems to produce. Most of the businesses pertain to the residents and not to non residents. It feels like Castro Valley’s economy is limited to shopping, restaurants, health care services, real estate, retirement planning, and family friendly entertainment. There is little else I see going on in Castro Valley. Hence, it feels like a resort which is designed to keep its residents from being concerned with the world outside of Castro Valley. Then again, I am probably exaggerating and saying things that could be proven untrue. I just said these things because that is what it often looks like from my point of view. It feels like a town which forces everyone in it to be vain, snarky, bratty, infantilized, child centric, and anti intellectual.

I will try to get at least one drumming session at my dad’s house in Castro Valley in per week. Perhaps I will also shower and do my laundry no more than three times per week. After how badly I have been stifled and deprived of space to hear myself because of Pig Gorl (Castro Valley’s mascot), I do not plan on sleeping in Castro Valley ever again.
