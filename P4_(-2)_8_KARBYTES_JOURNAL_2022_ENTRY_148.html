Once I got back to Castro Valley after riding the BART train out of Dublin, I felt too irked to talk to myself out loud. Once I got to the Chipotle parking lot while walking up Wilbeam, I popped exactly one THC pill and continued walking northward up Santa Maria, San Miguel, and then that church which has the cozy little (well lit) nook behind some bushes and electrical outlets on the other side. Though I was doubtful at first that this would not be the best place to crash land for a while, I am glad that I had good voices telling me hints which encouraged me to go to this nook instead of the area near the golf course on Redwood Road. As soon as I got here, I felt like I found my center of narrative gravity; my realest sense of what home is at this time. My dad’s house where I keep my stuff, take showers, do laundry, and play the drums is only about a twenty minute walk north of here. My mom’s house is about a 45 walk west of here. I am surrounded by familiar places and a sense that I belong and that I actually fit in here. As I laid on my side staring at ants walking and stopping briefly to exchange signals with each other, I got engrossed in thought and become more and more relaxed (and sane). I got a brilliant idea…

The pink bin has the potential to grow indefinitely. (Hold up; I got to put on my headphones because someone around the corner is talking and my mind is interpreting it as them scolding me. I do not really mind. Go ahead and say whatever you like and do whatever you like. I feel like I am 100% adapted to living outside. These people are smoking cigarettes also. Oh well. Those guys seem pissier than I am. I feel smugly serene and shameless for a change).

Anyway. What was I saying? Oh, yes. What I was saying is that the pink bin does not have a maximum size. Hence, if I am not careful, I could end up adding pages to it indefinitely and waste my time and server space on grouchy, prejudiced, misinformed, trite, depressing, and distracting crap instead of on the good stuff (which is what the green bin is supposed to represent).

At this point, I cannot easily define what characterizes the green bin because there is no green bin content yet; but I can be abstract and say that the green bin is all about having a good time doing science, philosophy, math, and art. I am afraid that Pig Gorl and others will try to prevent me from having a good time doing such things. Hence, perhaps the real theme of the green bin is simply doing science, philosophy, math, and art to the best of my ability.

Speaking of Pig Gorl, she is back and making her characteristic shrill protesting noises. I hope this page is the last page which I add to the pink bin. After I publish this page, I intend to delete some “ugly” pictures from my Instagram and back up the images I intend to add to my Instagram once I reset it with new content which includes images from Karlina Object “blog posts”.
